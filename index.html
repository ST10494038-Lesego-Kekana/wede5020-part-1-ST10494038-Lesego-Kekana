History

Lesego’s Artisan Bakery started in 2022. It was created by Lesego Sonia Kekana, who was only 19 years old at the time, located at 23 Magwa Cres St, Halfway House, 1682. She always loved baking and dreamed of owning her own bakery one day. At first, Lesego baked cookies and muffins at home and sold them at school. But selling at school was not allowed, so she had to stop. Still, she didn’t give up. She started selling her baked goods on weekends at taxi ranks and on the streets. Lesego saved the money she made and used it to open her first bakery. Sadly, the store burned down, and she lost everything. But the people in her community helped her with donations, and she was able to open a new store. This time, the bakery was a success. Today, Lesego’s Artisan Bakery is known for tasty baked goods and for the strong young woman who never gave up on her dream.

Mission and Vision

Our mission is to bake fresh, handmade treats that bring joy to our community, while inspiring others to never give up on their dreams. We strongly believe in hard work and kindness to empower others. Our vision is to grow Lesego’s Artisan Bakery into a well-known brand that supports young entrepreneurs and creates jobs through every bite. We see a future where our bakery is more than a shop—it’s a symbol of strength, creativity, and community love.
